What changes price

The estimate should explain the roof, not just the total.

SMI keeps pricing tied to visible conditions and photos. That means you can see why the scope includes a repair, replacement, coating, claim item, or upgrade before approving work.

Find the leak source first

Water can enter at one point and show up somewhere else inside the home. SMI traces roof penetrations, flashing, valleys, shingles, and attic clues before pricing the repair.

Avoid repeat service calls

A cheap repair is not a win if the wrong source is patched. The written scope should explain what is being repaired and what is being watched.

Know when replacement is smarter

If the roof is old, brittle, storm-damaged, or leaking in several areas, replacement may cost more now but less than repeated temporary repairs.

Pricing FAQs

Questions homeowners ask before booking.

Steep access, active leaks, rotten decking, valley work, chimney or wall flashing, specialty shingles, emergency tarping, multiple repair areas, and interior water damage can all move a repair above a basic service call.
A targeted repair is usually cheaper when the roof is otherwise healthy. Replacement becomes smarter when age, widespread granule loss, repeated leaks, storm damage, or failing decking make small repairs a short-term patch.
Emergency tarping or after-hours response can cost more because the crew is stopping active damage quickly. SMI explains the emergency protection scope first, then separates temporary protection from permanent repair.
Insurance may cover repair when wind, hail, fallen limbs, or another covered event caused the damage. Age, wear, deferred maintenance, and installation defects are usually handled differently by insurers.
