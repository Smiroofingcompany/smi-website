Food Processing and Industrial Roofing in Clarksville

Food processing facilities — including Tyson Foods' significant presence in the Clarksville area — present specific roofing challenges. Large roof spans, significant thermal cycling from processing heat, moisture exposure, and the need for uninterrupted operations during reroofing all require a contractor with proven commercial experience.

For processing and industrial buildings in Johnson County Industrial Park and similar facilities, SMI recommends metal panel roofing systems as the primary solution. Standing seam metal manages thermal movement through concealed clips, provides exceptional longevity, and requires minimal maintenance over a 30-40 year service life. Exposed-fastener panels with high-quality sealant systems are available where cost efficiency is the priority.

Unilever's Clarksville operations and similar light industrial facilities are well-suited to TPO single-ply membrane on flat and low-slope applications — providing energy efficiency, strong chemical resistance, and heat-welded seam integrity that outperforms older built-up systems.

University of the Ozarks, with its historic campus of approximately 700 students in the heart of Clarksville, represents a specialized roofing market. Johnson Regional Medical Center and other healthcare facilities in Clarksville share similar requirements: no interruption to patient care, strict odor and dust containment, and absolute weathertightness throughout the project.
