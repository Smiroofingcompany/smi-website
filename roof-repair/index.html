Fast, Reliable Roof Repair in Arkansas

Not every roof problem requires a full replacement. Leaks, missing shingles, damaged flashing, worn pipe boots, and sagging gutters can often be repaired quickly and affordably. SMI Roofing provides honest assessments and straightforward repairs. If a repair will solve your problem, we'll tell you. If your roof needs replacement, we'll tell you that too.

With 1,700+ roofs completed and 231+ five-star reviews, we've built our reputation on doing the right thing for every homeowner, even when the right thing means a $300 repair instead of a $12,000 replacement.

Common Roof Repairs We Handle

Leak repair: Active leaks need immediate attention. Water intrusion causes mold, wood rot, damaged insulation, and ceiling stains that only get worse with time. We locate the source of the leak, not just the symptom, and fix it permanently.

Missing or damaged shingles: Wind, falling branches, and age can displace or crack individual shingles. We match replacement shingles as closely as possible to your existing roof and install them to manufacturer specifications.

Flashing repair: Flashing around chimneys, walls, skylights, and valleys is the most common failure point on otherwise healthy roofs. We remove deteriorated flashing, install new step or counter flashing, and seal all transitions properly.

Pipe boot replacement: The rubber boots around plumbing vent pipes crack and deteriorate over time, creating leak paths. We replace them with new boots that seal properly and last.

Ridge cap repair: Ridge caps take the most wind abuse on any roof. We replace blown-off or damaged ridge caps and ensure proper ventilation is maintained.

Gutter repair and replacement: Sagging, leaking, or detached gutters cause water damage to fascia, soffit, and foundation. We repair or replace gutters and ensure proper drainage away from your home.

Free Roof Inspections

Every repair starts with a thorough inspection. We climb your roof, document conditions with photos, and give you an honest assessment of what needs attention now, what can wait, and what your overall roof timeline looks like. There's no charge and no pressure. If your roof is in good shape, we'll tell you that and shake your hand on the way out.

We recommend annual inspections for every home, plus an additional inspection after any significant storm event. Catching small problems early prevents expensive repairs later.

When to Repair vs. Replace

General rule: if your roof is under 15 years old and the damage is isolated to specific areas, repair is usually the right call. If your roof is approaching 20-25 years and you're seeing widespread wear, granule loss, curling, or multiple leak points, replacement becomes the more cost-effective long-term decision. We walk you through the math for your specific situation so you can make an informed choice.
