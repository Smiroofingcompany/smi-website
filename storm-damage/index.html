How Storms Damage Your Roof

Hail damage is the most common storm-related roofing issue in Arkansas. Hailstones as small as one inch can crack shingles, dislodge granules, and compromise the waterproof seal. The damage often isn't visible from the ground, which is why a professional inspection after any hailstorm is critical.

Wind damage from severe thunderstorms and tornadoes can lift shingles, tear off ridge caps, expose underlayment, and in extreme cases, remove entire sections of roofing. Arkansas regularly experiences straight-line winds exceeding 60 mph during spring and summer storms.

Fallen trees and debris can puncture decking, crush trusses, and cause structural damage that requires immediate attention to prevent interior water damage.

Driving rain exploits any existing vulnerability in your roof system. Even small gaps caused by wind-lifted shingles can allow water intrusion that leads to mold, rot, and ceiling damage within days.

Our Storm Damage Response Process

Emergency tarping: If your roof is actively leaking or exposed, we can deploy emergency tarps to prevent further interior damage. Call us at (501) 464-5139 and we'll get a crew to your home as quickly as possible.

Thorough inspection: We inspect every plane of your roof, all flashings, ridge caps, valleys, pipe boots, and gutters. We document everything with high-resolution photos and detailed notes.

Insurance claim filing: We provide a complete damage report that you can submit to your insurance company. We meet your adjuster on-site, walk the roof with them, and make sure every area of damage is documented and included in the claim.

Supplement process: If your insurance company's initial estimate doesn't cover the full scope of necessary repairs, we submit supplements with additional documentation. We've successfully supplemented hundreds of claims to get homeowners the coverage their policies provide.

Repair or replacement: Once your claim is approved, we complete the work promptly. Most storm damage repairs take one to three days. Full replacements take one to two days. You pay your deductible and insurance covers the rest.

Don't Wait After a Storm

Insurance companies have filing deadlines. In Arkansas, most homeowner policies require you to report damage within one year, but the sooner you file, the stronger your claim. Waiting allows further deterioration that can complicate the process or give your insurer grounds to deny coverage.

We offer free storm damage inspections with no obligation. If we find damage, we help you file. If your roof is fine, we tell you it's fine. That's the SMI difference: honesty first, always.
